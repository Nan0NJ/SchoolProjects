GAME INFORMATION & RULES

Objective

The objective of MineSweeper is to clear all the mines from the game board without detonating any of them. The game board consists of a grid of cells, each of which can either contain a mine or be safe. Your goal is to identify the locations of the mines and mark them with a flag, while clearing the safe cells.
To begin playing, click on a cell on the game board. If the cell is safe, a number will appear, indicating how many mines are adjacent to that cell. If the cell contains a mine, the game is over. You can mark a cell as containing a mine by right-clicking on it, which will place a flag. The objective is to mark all the cells that contain mines and clear all the safe cells without detonating any mines.

Scoring

Your score in Minesweeper is based on the time taken to complete a level, the number of mines cleared, and the number of flags used. The faster you complete a level with fewer flags used, the higher your score will be. You can also earn bonus points by completing levels without detonating any mines.

Conclusion

Minesweeper is a classic computer game that has been around since the early 1990s. The game is played on a square grid, where each square represents either a mine or a safe space. The goal of the game is to clear all the safe spaces without detonating any of the hidden mines.
Overall, Minesweeper has become a beloved classic in the world of computer gaming. Despite its simplicity, the game continues to capture the imagination of players around the world.
